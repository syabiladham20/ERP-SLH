Strategic Recommendation: Value of Professional Deployment
Enhancing Web Performance & Operational Intelligence

Moving from the current free-tier configuration to a professional deployment is not merely a technical upgrade; it is a strategic investment in operational stability and data-driven decision making.

1. Web Performance: Free vs. Paid
  • Free Version (Current): Relies on SQLite and shared CPU resources. During peak hours (morning data entry), multiple users attempting to save logs simultaneously will encounter "Database Locked" errors and timeouts (>10s latency).
  • Professional Deployment: Utilizes PostgreSQL and dedicated web workers. This architecture supports parallel processing, ensuring <1s response times even with 50+ concurrent users across 30 houses.
2. Cost-Benefit Analysis

The cost of upgrading (estimated $7 - $15 / month) is negligible compared to the operational risks of system downtime.

  • Reliability: 99.9% uptime ensures data is always accessible.
  • Risk Mitigation: The cost of one unrecorded mortality event or missed feed adjustment due to system unavailability far exceeds the monthly subscription fee.
  • Scalability: Prevents the need for a costly "emergency migration" later when the database hits file-size limits.
3. Database Integrity & Traceability

Tracking the flow from Farm to Hatchery requires strict transactional integrity that SQLite struggles to maintain under load.

PostgreSQL ensures that every Hatchability record is inextricably linked to its source Flock and Daily Log. This robust linkage allows for deep root-cause analysis: tracing poor chick quality back to specific feed batches or environmental conditions weeks prior.

4. Speed of Preventive Action

Speed saves lives. A slow dashboard discourages frequent checking. By reducing load times from ~10s to ~3s, management can monitor flocks in real-time.

Immediate visibility into a 0.5% mortality spike allows for intervention (e.g., adjusting ventilation) before it escalates into a major loss event. The system shifts from a "reporting tool" to an "alerting system."

5. Historical Analysis & Prediction

Data is an asset that grows in value over time. SQLite performance degrades as historical data accumulates. PostgreSQL is designed for terabytes of data.

A professional database allows keeping 5+ years of detailed flock history online without performance loss. This enables powerful year-over-year benchmarking (e.g., "How does the 2026 Spring flock compare to the 2024 record holder?") and predictive modeling for future flock performance.

Limitations of the Free Plan for Production
  • Database Concurrency: SQLite allows only one writer at a time. With multiple users entering data simultaneously, the system will frequently lock ("Database Locked" errors), causing data loss and frustration.
  • CPU Throttling: The 100-second CPU limit is insufficient for generating complex ISO and Hatchery reports. Once exceeded, the site becomes extremely slow or unresponsive until the next day.
  • No Automated Backups: The Free plan does not support "Always-on tasks," meaning we cannot run automated nightly database backups. This poses a significant risk to data safety.
  • Storage Limits: The 512MB limit will be quickly exhausted by daily logs and photos from 30 flocks, potentially freezing the application.
